Why Frate

Honestly, we think we can blow this place up.

We do not say that lightly. We have worked with a lot of Newcastle venues and Frate is sitting on one of the strongest organic content opportunities in the city right now. Here is why.

The venue is already the set

Open-air courtyard, shipping containers, fairy lights, DJs, street food, summer sun, winter coats and hot drinks. Every corner of Frate is already a piece of content. Most venues have to build an aesthetic. You have one. That is worth more than most people realise. It means every video starts at a baseline of "looks incredible" before a single person steps into frame.

The audience and the format are perfectly aligned

Students, young professionals, groups hunting for somewhere different on a Friday night. That whole demographic now decides where to go based on a 15-second video. Frate's whole proposition, food, drinks, music, courtyard, events, is exactly the kind of thing that performs on Reels and TikTok. It is high-energy, visual, and social. The algorithm loves this kind of content. We just need to feed it.

Every night is a content opportunity

Unlike most venues that have to manufacture moments, Frate creates them without trying. A DJ set, a cinema night, a record fayre, a sports crowd, a stag group, a bank holiday crush, a couple on a first date in the corner. Every one of those is a Reel. We turn up, capture it, and turn it around fast. Over a couple of months that compounds into a feed that looks alive, not staged.

Events deserve a run-up, not just a poster

Every event at Frate is a chance to run a mini content campaign: tease it, show up on the night, recap afterwards. That is three or four pieces of content per event, building anticipation and capturing the moment, instead of one static graphic the day before that nobody sees.

The ceiling here is huge

New Greek is a single restaurant on Chillingham Road. Over 45 million views and 6,000+ new followers. Frate has more visual range, more personalities, more events, and sits in the middle of Newcastle's night-out route. There is no ceiling on what this could become. With the right content engine behind it, Frate should be the venue other cities copy, not the one trying to catch up.

Content Concepts

8 videos we think you should try that could go viral

These are starting points, not a fixed list. Half of the best videos we make come out of things that happen on the night.

01

How drunk is this person?

Show punters a photo or clip of someone in the courtyard, let them guess how drunk they are, then breathalyse the person and reveal the score. Reaction-driven, high replay value, perfect for a Saturday night crowd.

02

How drunk are you?

Ask people at the bar how drunk they think they are, breathalyse them, and catch their reaction when the number hits. Works every weekend, builds a recurring format people tune in for.

03

Summer at Frate

A rolling showcase of what is coming up. DJ sets, cinema nights, bank holiday takeovers, record fayres, sports days. Turns the feed into the "what is on this weekend" guide for the city.

04

Tell us X for free entry or a drink

Short on-the-gate prompts. Name three things on the menu, tell us your worst date, sing a line of a song, do a Geordie impression. Instant comedy, instant engagement, gets people sharing with mates before they even arrive.

05

Best pick up lines

Stop lads in the courtyard. "What is your best pick up line?" Answers range from gold to tragic. Always pulls views, always gets shared, fits Frate's social, group-night energy perfectly.

06

What is on the menu

Fast, close-up, trending-audio shots of the food and drinks. Schwarma wraps being built, pizza slices coming out the oven, pints being pulled, cocktails being made. The kind of content people screenshot and send to the group chat.

07

Weirdest drink requests

Bar staff telling the camera the strangest orders they have ever had. Bar staff are underrated characters. Give them a minute of screen time and they carry the whole feed.

08

Whatever is trending

Weekly pickups of whatever format is exploding on TikTok and Reels that week, adapted to Frate. Speed is the edge here. We move on a trend within 48 hours, not two weeks. This is the category that wins virality.
