Ready-Made Content
6 Video Ideas You Could Film on Day One

These are formats that are performing right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. Every single one plays directly to Elephant Rock's strengths. The beachfront location, the seafood, the sunset, the story.

Idea 01
"Sunset cocktails with an ocean view"
Sunset Cocktails Most Shareable

Golden hour at Elephant Rock. A cocktail being placed on the table with the ocean behind it. The sun dropping towards the horizon. Slow, cinematic shots. The glass catching the light. Maybe a second cocktail arrives. No voiceover needed. Just the visuals, a moody audio track, and one line of text on screen: "This is real. Roker Beach."

Why it works: Sunset plus cocktails plus ocean is the single most shareable format on social media. People save these videos, send them to friends, and plan visits around them. This is the kind of content that makes someone in Newcastle or Durham say "we need to go there this weekend." It is aspirational, beautiful, and effortlessly positions Elephant Rock as a destination.
Idea 02
"The sound of fresh seafood hitting the plate"
ASMR Seafood Plating

Close-up of seafood being plated. The sizzle, the clink of the plate, the drizzle of sauce. Extreme close-ups of textures. Prawns, oysters, crab. The camera pulls back to reveal the full plate with the ocean visible through the window behind it. Pure ASMR food content.

Why it works: ASMR food content consistently outperforms every other format. The sounds of cooking and plating trigger a sensory response that keeps people watching until the end. High watch time means the algorithm pushes the video to more people. Seafood is particularly effective because of the variety of sounds and textures involved.
Idea 03
"From old weather shelter to beachfront restaurant"
Transformation Story Community

Before-and-after footage of the building. The old weather shelter on Marine Walk. Then the transformation. Construction progress. The reveal of the finished restaurant. End with a wide shot of Elephant Rock open and buzzing with customers, the sea in the background. Text overlay: "This used to be a weather shelter."

Why it works: Transformation content is one of the highest-performing formats across all platforms. People cannot resist watching before-and-after reveals. This video also connects with the Sunderland community. Locals will recognise the old shelter and feel a genuine sense of pride and curiosity about what it has become. It drives comments, shares, and local buzz.
Idea 04
"New season. New menu. Same view."
Seasonal Menu Reveal Recurring

Each time the menu changes, film a cinematic reveal. Quick cuts of new dishes being plated and served. The ocean view stays constant in every shot, anchoring the brand. End with a slow pan across the full table of new dishes. "The menu changed. The view did not."

Why it works: Seasonal menu reveals give you a natural content calendar. Each one creates anticipation and a reason for people to come back. The recurring format builds brand recognition, and the ocean view becomes Elephant Rock's visual signature. People start associating that view with your food, which is exactly what you want.
Idea 05
"Morning coffee on the beach. Evening cocktails on the beach."
Lifestyle Beachfront All-Day

Split the video in two. First half: early morning, calm sea, a coffee on the table, soft light. Second half: same table, same view, but now it is evening. Cocktails, warm lighting, the buzz of a full restaurant. The contrast between the two moods shows Elephant Rock as an all-day destination, not just a dinner spot.

Why it works: Lifestyle content positions Elephant Rock as more than a restaurant. It becomes a place, an experience. The morning-to-evening format shows range and gives people multiple reasons to visit. It also performs well because the visual contrast between the two halves keeps people watching to see the shift. This is the kind of content that defines a brand.
Idea 06
"We took them to a secret beachfront restaurant"
Reactions Customers Discovery

Film customers arriving and reacting to the venue for the first time. The moment they see the view. The moment the food arrives. Genuine reactions, no scripts. Cut between multiple groups throughout an evening. End with someone saying they did not even know this place existed. "Neither did we, until now."

Why it works: Reaction content is authentic and relatable. When people see real customers genuinely impressed, it builds trust in a way that polished marketing cannot. The "hidden gem" angle is powerful, especially for a new venue that most people have not discovered yet. It creates a sense of urgency and FOMO that drives visits.
