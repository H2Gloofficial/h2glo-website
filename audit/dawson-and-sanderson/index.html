Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Dawson & Sanderson has a lot going for it. The gaps are not about the product, the branches, or the service. They are about how the story is being told online. Fix these and bookings will follow.

1

Instagram Is Underpowered Relative to the Brand

Around 4,900 Instagram followers for a 60-plus year travel brand with a branch network and 8 million customers is well below where it should be. The feed leans on static destination photos and deal graphics. Nice to look at, but they do not stop the scroll and they do not get pushed by the algorithm in 2026.

The fix is a shift from "pretty beach photo with price tag" to video-led content with personality. Consultants on camera, customer reactions, destination POVs. You already have the audience intent. People research holidays on Instagram constantly. You just need content that actually gets picked up when they do.

2

TikTok Is a Near-Empty Platform Where Your Customer Is Actively Searching

"Where should I go on holiday 2026", "cheap all inclusive", "best cruise for first timers". These are high-intent TikTok searches happening right now, and almost no UK high street travel agents are showing up in the results. That is an enormous organic discovery gap sitting wide open.

One Dawson & Sanderson consultant doing a 30-second "three countries you can get for under £500 this summer" could pull six-figure views overnight. The under-45 traveller, the exact audience that thinks high street agents are outdated, is on TikTok searching for exactly what you sell. Every month without a TikTok presence is free leads going to competitors.

3

No Face, No Expert, No Personality on Camera

Travel in 2026 is a trust-driven purchase, and trust is built through faces. Right now, the Dawson & Sanderson content does not have a recognisable on-screen consultant. No "Sarah at the Gosforth branch". No "ask Mark about Caribbean cruises". The brand feels like a company rather than a team, and that is a real gap in a category where people want advice from someone they have seen before.

The fix is simple. Pick two or three consultants from across the branches. Put them on camera weekly. Let them talk about what they know, the destinations they have been to, the mistakes they stop customers making. Build them into mini personalities. That is how you turn a 60-year brand into a modern content engine.

4

Review Narratives on Trustpilot Need Active Countering

Branch Google reviews are excellent, but Trustpilot sits at 4.0 and contains real complaints about post-booking communication and accommodation mismatches. Prospective customers read Trustpilot before they book. Those reviews are currently shaping first impressions with no counter-narrative.

The answer is not to argue online. It is to create content that shows the opposite. Behind-the-scenes of your duty consultant team handling an in-resort issue. A customer video diary of a holiday that went right. Transparent "here is what happens if something goes wrong" explainers. Content like that rewrites the narrative faster than any Trustpilot reply ever could.

5

The Branch Network Is Being Used as Infrastructure, Not Content

Every branch is a film set. Gosforth, South Shields, Ashington, Doncaster, Redcar, Middlesbrough. Every one of them has consultants, customers walking in, brochures, maps, travel money desks, and the exact look and feel that builds trust on camera. Right now, almost none of that is being captured.

A weekly in-branch content day across the network could produce 40 pieces of video a month. Consultant picks of the week, customer booking moments, overheard conversations, "what a £2k budget actually gets you" desk walkthroughs. The infrastructure is already in place and already paid for. It just needs a camera and a plan.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels. All of them are built around what makes Dawson & Sanderson unique. Film any one of these properly in a branch and it could outperform everything you have posted in the last year.

Idea 01
"POV: You walk into Dawson & Sanderson and ask for a surprise holiday under £500"
POV Destination Reveal High Hook

Start on a customer at the branch door. Walk up to the desk. Text overlay: "I have given this travel agent £500 and told them to send me anywhere." Consultant on camera smirks, flips open the screen, and starts pitching three destinations with quick cutaways to beach, pool, plate of food, sunset. End on the consultant sliding a brochure across the desk with the winning destination revealed. Trending audio over the top.

Why it works: Destination reveal content is one of the top-performing formats on TikTok travel. The under-£500 hook is relatable, the in-branch setting is native to Dawson & Sanderson specifically, and the suspense of "where are they going to send me" keeps people watching to the end. One strong version of this could hit seven-figure views.
Idea 02
"What £500 vs £1,000 vs £2,000 actually gets you in Turkey right now"
Comparison Value Expert

A consultant at the desk with three brochures laid out. £500, £1,000, £2,000. For each budget, a quick cutaway to the hotel, the pool, the beach, the flight time, all ticked off in captions. Honest commentary on what you get and what you do not. End with a direct camera line: "Whatever your budget is, come in and we will find it." Can be repeated for Greece, Spain, Caribbean, Dubai and so on as a whole series.

Why it works: Comparison content performs because it answers a question every holiday shopper has: "Is it worth paying more?" It positions Dawson & Sanderson as the expert who knows the difference between £500 and £2,000 hotels, something booking engines cannot do. It also drives branch footfall, because the natural next step is "come in and we will build your exact budget".
Idea 03
"Three things a travel agent knows that Booking.com will never tell you"
Expert Tips Authority Series

Consultant at the desk straight to camera. Three quick, punchy secrets. For example: the room numbers you should ask for in a specific Turkish resort, which Caribbean island is a nightmare in November, the cruise cabin category that is always upgraded for free. Fast cuts between each tip with on-screen text. End on "That is what 60 years of travel advice looks like. Book with us in branch or online."

Why it works: Insider information content is huge on TikTok and Reels because it feels like genuine value, not a sales pitch. It directly positions the Dawson & Sanderson consultant as an expert worth listening to, which is the core of the brand. It is a format you can run weekly forever, and every consultant across the branch network can contribute their own tips. Endless content supply.
Idea 04
"Filming customers booking their 2026 holiday at the desk"
Customer Moments UGC Style Social Proof

Real customers, at the branch desk, the moment they commit to a trip. Capture the reactions. The grin when the flights are locked in. The hug between couples booking an anniversary. The kid losing it when Disney is confirmed. Each clip is 10 to 15 seconds. Cut a compilation together every week with captions like "Today at Dawson & Sanderson Gosforth". Add a trending audio underneath.

Why it works: Real customer reaction content is the most persuasive form of social proof on short-form video. It tells prospective bookers "this is what happens when you walk through our door". It humanises a 60-year brand and directly shows the emotional payoff of booking in a branch, which is exactly the thing Booking.com cannot replicate. Needs a simple consent process, then it becomes a weekly staple.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Launch a Consultant-Led TikTok Account

Pick two or three consultants across the branch network and build the TikTok account around them as the face of Dawson & Sanderson. Destination reveals, budget comparisons, booking moments, expert tips. Post three to five times a week. The brand TikTok account is the single biggest untapped growth lever you have.

  • Name and tag specific consultants so viewers recognise them across videos
  • Use trending audio to accelerate reach in the early weeks
  • Geo-target captions and hashtags to Newcastle, North East and Yorkshire audiences
  • Repurpose every TikTok to Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts on the same day

The reality: Almost no UK high street travel agent is winning on TikTok yet. The first one to own destination content in 2026 will dominate the "book with a real person" search for years. That should be Dawson & Sanderson.

Build a Branch Content Day System

One day a month per branch with a content producer on site. Batch-film two to four weeks of content in a single day. Consultant tips, destination walkthroughs, desk reveals, customer booking clips with consent. The branch network becomes a content factory without pulling consultants off the phones.

  • Produce a branch-specific content calendar every month
  • Rotate featured destinations to match your commercial campaigns
  • Mix evergreen "expert tips" content with timely deal and launch content
  • Use branch content to feed paid social ads targeting local postcodes

Convert Feefo and Branch Google Reviews Into Video Social Proof

Feefo Platinum and 4.6 to 4.9 Google ratings are powerful but invisible outside the review sites themselves. Turn the best written reviews into short video quotes with destination b-roll. Film customer video testimonials post-holiday. Embed everything on the website and use it in paid ads.

  • Pick the 20 best reviews from Feefo and Google and film them as 15-second quote videos
  • Offer customers a small thank-you for filming a 30-second post-holiday video
  • Add review social proof prominently to the website homepage and branch pages
  • Use customer reaction content in Meta and Google ads to cut cost per booking

Counter the Trustpilot Gap With Transparency Content

Trustpilot at 4.0 with complaints about post-booking communication is shaping first impressions. The answer is a "what happens after you book" content series. Show the resort support team, show how issues get handled, show the duty team that is on call at 2am. Transparency rebuilds trust faster than a pinned reply ever will.

Quick win: A single three-minute explainer video on the homepage titled "what happens after you book with Dawson & Sanderson" would counter most of the concerns a nervous Trustpilot reader arrives with.
