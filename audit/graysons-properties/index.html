Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Graysons has the stock, the service range, and the footprint. The gaps are not about the business. They are about how the story is being told online. Fix these and the enquiries will follow.

1

490 Instagram Followers Is Sitting Well Below Potential

490 followers for an established letting agent operating across Newcastle, Jesmond, Sunderland and Durham is low. The content is mostly static property shots and listing tiles. It looks like a shop window, but social media is not a shop window. Static listings belong on Rightmove. Instagram and Reels reward personality, tours, and people on camera.

The fix is a weekly Reels cadence built around property tours, staff on camera, and area guides for the postcodes you actually list in. Done consistently, getting to 5,000 engaged followers within six months is very achievable for an agent in this market.

2

TikTok Is the Single Biggest Missed Opportunity

Student housing is one of the most viral categories on TikTok. "Newcastle student house tour," "what 500 quid gets you in Jesmond," "Leazes Terrace flat tour" are all searches racking up hundreds of thousands of views. You have a TikTok, but it is barely moving. A handful of videos, very low follower count, no consistent format.

A Newcastle or Northumbria student looking for a house in September 2026 is looking at TikTok, not Rightmove. The first letting agent to properly own "Newcastle student house tours" on TikTok will fill their properties every year without ever running a paid ad. Right now, nobody in the city is doing this properly.

3

No Video House Tours, Despite the Format Being Proven

Vertical short-form house tours are the single highest-performing content format in UK property right now. A 45-second Jesmond flat tour with a hook at the second mark, a walk-through, a price reveal, and a CTA in the caption is the exact video that drives tenant enquiries. Yet your listings are being marketed almost entirely through photos.

Every new instruction should get a video tour filmed the same day the photography is done. That single habit change would give you 4 to 6 pieces of high-intent content every single week, with zero extra content days needed.

4

Nothing Shows the People Behind Graysons

Students and young renters do not sign contracts with logos. They sign with people they feel they can trust. Right now, the audience has no idea who the agents at Graysons are, what you sound like, or whether you are the kind of landlord who will answer the phone at 9pm when the boiler packs in.

"Day in the life of a Newcastle letting agent" style content is one of the most under-used formats in UK property. Put your negotiators, property managers, and directors on camera. Reviewers on Google and WhichPad go in harder on faceless companies. People defend and recommend people. Show who runs Graysons.

5

The Student Booking Window Is Not Being Engineered

Newcastle and Northumbria students start looking for next year's house in October and November. Peak sign-up is November through January. That is a 90-day window every single year when the right content puts you in front of thousands of actively searching renters, and the wrong content means you are invisible.

There is no visible content calendar aligned to that window. No "still deciding on your house?" countdown content, no group content targeting "tag the 4 mates you are moving in with," no Jesmond vs Heaton vs Sandyford comparison reels. A proper student-season content plan could genuinely change how quickly your portfolio fills each year.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats built specifically for a North East letting agent, not generic property content. All four can be filmed around your existing portfolio and team. Post any one of these properly and it will outperform 90 percent of what UK agents are putting out right now.

Idea 01
"The reveal walk-through: this Jesmond flat looks tiny until…"
House Tour Reveal Hook-First

Start on the front door. Tight hallway, ordinary exterior. "You would never guess what is behind this door." Walk in, pan up to high ceilings, throw open the living room doors. Bigger than expected, period features, natural light. End on the rent per person per week and the line "available September, only 4 rooms left." Trending audio under it.

Why it works: Reveal content is the highest-performing format on Reels and TikTok in 2026. Property especially. The viewer gets hooked on "I want to see what is inside." Price at the end drives the enquiry. One of these done well on Leazes Terrace or Grosvenor Gardens could do 500k+ views.
Idea 02
"What does 500 quid a month actually get you in Newcastle?"
Price Compare Student Searchable

Pick three real properties at different price points from your portfolio. A 400 per month room in Sandyford, a 500 per month room in Jesmond, a 600 per month ensuite near the universities. Walk through each with a running price banner on screen. Quick hits: kitchen, bedroom, the view, the commute to campus. End with a clear CTA: "DM GRAYSONS for the shortlist."

Why it works: "What does X get you in Y city" is one of the most searched formats in property TikTok. Students actively look this up when deciding their budget. It positions Graysons as the answer to the exact question they are already typing. Totally repeatable every month with different stock.
Idea 03
"Day in the life of a Newcastle letting agent in peak season"
Day in Life Staff Trust

Follow one of your negotiators through a morning of back-to-back viewings. 8am unlocking Leazes Terrace, 10am showing four Northumbria students around a 5 bed, 1pm quick lunch at Quay, 3pm chasing a gas certificate, 5pm signing a tenancy. Fast cuts, genuine moments, a bit of humour. Show the actual pace of the job during student season.

Why it works: Day in the life content humanises the brand and builds trust before a tenant ever walks in the office. Students comparing Graysons against a faceless competitor will lean toward the agency they feel they already know. It is also endlessly repeatable across different staff members and seasons.
Idea 04
"Before and after: how we turned this tired 5 bed into a fully booked student house"
Before / After Staging Social Proof

Pick one property you have recently refreshed. Open with the before footage. Tired wallpaper, dated kitchen, empty bedrooms. Quick cut to the work being done. Then the reveal: freshly painted, styled, photographed, ready to market. End card: "listed Monday, fully let by Friday." Text overlay throughout so it works with sound off.

Why it works: Before and after is one of the most saved and shared formats on Instagram Reels. For Graysons it doubles as social proof to landlords: "this is what we can do with your property." One video pulls in tenant enquiries and landlord instructions at the same time. Two revenue streams, one piece of content.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Build a Student-Season Content Calendar

The student sign-up window runs October to January. That 90-day stretch is when your content has to be at its loudest. Right now there is no visible seasonal plan, so the content is either absent or off-pace.

  • Map 12 weeks of content around the sign-up window: tours in October, price comparisons in November, group viewing content in December, final decision content in January
  • Run location-based Reels: Jesmond vs Heaton vs Sandyford, walking commute to Newcastle Uni, nearest takeaway to Northumbria
  • Use paid Reels ads geo-targeted to Newcastle and Northumbria students during the booking window
  • Repeat the entire cycle every year with fresh stock. The playbook compounds

Quick win: Film every new instruction as a vertical video the same day the photographer attends. That single habit gives you a running content library with zero extra production days.

Fix the Split Facebook and Handle Consistency Issue

You currently have two Facebook pages (GraysonsPropertiesTeam and graysonspropertyletting), the Instagram is @graysonspropertysolutions, the TikTok is @graysonproperties, and Twitter is @graysonsne. That is four different handles across four platforms. For a brand that lives on trust, it is confusing to tenants and landlords trying to check you out.

  • Pick one handle, ideally @graysonsproperties, and unify as many platforms as possible
  • Consolidate the two Facebook pages into one active page and archive the other
  • Use the same profile image and bio line across every platform to create a recognisable visual signature
  • Add all handles to your website footer and email signatures so tenants can actually find you

Turn Your Landlords Into a Marketing Channel

Landlords trust Graysons with significant assets. Short video case studies of landlords you already work with would be the single most persuasive piece of new-business content you could make. Two minutes of a real landlord explaining why they switched to Graysons would outperform any Google Ad you could run.

  • Film 3 to 5 short landlord testimonial videos across sales and lettings
  • Cut each into a 60-second Reel plus a 2 minute long-form version for the website
  • Pair with a clear "list your property with Graysons" landing page and CTA

Start Owning Newcastle Property Search on Google and TikTok

"Student houses Newcastle," "letting agents Jesmond," "flats to rent NE1" are the exact search terms your next tenants are typing. Your Rightmove feed ranks for listings, but your own brand visibility in those searches is thin. Content and SEO together should move that.

The reality: Your competitors are fighting for search results. They are not fighting for TikTok. Whichever Newcastle agent owns the TikTok student house tour space in 2026 will dominate student lettings in this city for the next 5 years. That should be Graysons.
