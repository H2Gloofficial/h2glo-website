Where The Opportunity Is
Five Things Holding You Back

The food is great, the concept is proven, and the brand has real personality. The challenge right now is that your digital presence is not keeping pace with your ambition. Here is where the biggest gaps are.

1

Your Website Has No Menu

Your website at thegiddygoose.co.uk is live but the menu section says "Coming Soon." For a restaurant, the menu page is the single most visited page after the homepage. When someone hears about The Giddy Goose and Googles you, the first thing they want to see is what you serve and what it costs. If they cannot find that in 10 seconds, they leave and try somewhere else.

Right now, a potential customer who finds your website has to either call you, check Just Eat, or give up. Every extra step loses people. A complete website with your full menu, prices, photos of the food, your bottomless brunch details, and a way to book or order should be the absolute foundation everything else builds on.

The website also does not mention your second location at Blackhall Mill, your opening hours are tucked away, and there is no online booking system visible. These are all friction points that cost you customers every single day.

2

No TikTok Presence

TikTok is the most powerful free marketing tool available to restaurants right now. It is not even close. And wood-fired pizza is one of the platform's top-performing food categories. Videos of dough being stretched, flames licking the base of a pizza, toppings bubbling, that perfect leopard-spotted crust coming out of the oven. These videos routinely get hundreds of thousands of views with zero ad spend.

You are in Consett. There is essentially zero competition from other local restaurants on TikTok. The first pizza place in the area to properly nail TikTok content will own the local food conversation. That should be you. Right now, potential customers in Consett, Stanley, and the surrounding villages are scrolling TikTok every evening deciding where to eat, and The Giddy Goose is not appearing in their feed.

One well-shot 30-second video of a pizza going into your wood-fired oven could reach 50,000 to 100,000 people in County Durham without spending a single penny. That is not a guess. That is what we see happen repeatedly for restaurant clients.

3

Instagram Is Not Working Hard Enough

You have an Instagram account at @giddygooserestaurant, which is good. But Instagram in 2026 rewards one thing above all else: Reels. Short-form video content. Static food photos do not get pushed to new audiences the way they used to. The algorithm has fundamentally changed.

For a restaurant like yours, Instagram should be generating a steady stream of new local followers who then convert into customers. That means posting Reels at least 4 to 5 times per week, using location tags, local hashtags, and trending audio. Your content should be making people in Consett and the surrounding area hungry and then giving them an immediate way to book or order.

The content is already happening in your kitchen every single day. Pizzas being made, burgers being stacked, wine being poured, a busy Friday night service. It just needs to be captured, edited into short engaging clips, and posted consistently.

4

The Bottomless Brunch Is Not Being Marketed

"Bottomless brunch" is one of the most searched terms for eating out across the UK. People actively look for it. They search "bottomless brunch near me" on Google, they search it on TikTok, they ask friends for recommendations. You have a genuinely strong offer: unlimited pizza, prosecco, pints, and house wine for 90 minutes with a complimentary first drink. That is better than what most places in Newcastle charge twice as much for.

But if nobody knows about it beyond your existing followers, it is not driving new customers through the door. This offer needs its own dedicated content series, its own highlight on Instagram, its own section on the website, and ideally its own landing page that ranks when someone searches "bottomless brunch Consett" or "bottomless brunch County Durham."

One TikTok video showing a group of friends arriving, unlimited pizzas hitting the table, prosecco flowing, everyone having a brilliant time, could drive bookings for months.

5

No Online Booking System

There is no visible way to book a table online. In 2026, a significant percentage of restaurant bookings happen outside of business hours. Someone is on their phone at 11pm deciding where to eat tomorrow night. If they cannot tap a button and book right then, they move on to somewhere they can. You lose that customer without ever knowing they were interested.

A simple online booking widget on your website and linked from your Instagram bio removes all friction. It also gives you data on how many covers to expect, reduces no-shows when you send confirmation messages, and makes your operation look professional and established. It does not need to be expensive. Tools like ResDiary, DesignMyNight, or even a simple form that sends you a notification would transform your booking flow overnight.

Ready-Made Content
5 Video Ideas You Could Film Today

These are formats that are working right now for restaurants on TikTok and Instagram. All of them play to The Giddy Goose's strengths as a wood-fired pizza restaurant with a brilliant story and an even better product.

Idea 01
"From a trailer to a restaurant in 12 months"
Origin Story Brand Building Emotional

Show old footage or photos of the trailer. The five-star reviews coming in. The decision to go permanent. Walking into the empty unit on Newmarket Street. First service. Packed Friday night. The second location opening. A 60-second story arc that makes people root for you.

Why it works: People love underdog stories. "Small business grows" content consistently outperforms polished corporate content on TikTok. It builds emotional connection, makes people want to support you, and gets shared because people tag friends saying "we need to go here." This single video could define your brand for the next year.
Idea 02
"Bottomless pizza and prosecco for 90 minutes. Here is what happened."
Experience Social Proof Viral Format

Film a group arriving for bottomless brunch. Time-lapse of pizzas coming to the table. Prosecco being poured. Everyone laughing and eating. Show how many pizzas the table got through. End with "Book yours. Link in bio." Fast cuts, upbeat audio, pure energy.

Why it works: "Bottomless" content is one of the most shared restaurant formats because it triggers curiosity and disbelief. People want to know if it is actually worth it, so they watch the whole video. Then they tag their friends. Then they book. This format drives direct revenue every time it is posted.
Idea 03
"POV: You are watching your pizza get made in a wood-fired oven"
ASMR Process Satisfying

Close-up of dough being stretched by hand. Sauce being ladled. Toppings going on. The pizza sliding into the oven on the peel. Flames wrapping around the base. Cheese bubbling. That leopard-spotted crust. Pulling it out. Slicing it. Steam rising. No voiceover. Just the sounds and a chill track.

Why it works: Wood-fired pizza process videos are mesmerising. They sit at the intersection of ASMR, food content, and satisfying videos, which are three of the biggest content categories on the platform. These videos get watched multiple times because they are genuinely relaxing. High watch time means the algorithm pushes them to more people. Simple as that.
Idea 04
"Rating our entire menu from 1 to 10 (owner edition)"
Personality Menu Showcase Engagement

You sit in front of the camera and rate every item on your own menu. Be honest. "This one is a 7, solid but not my favourite. This one is a 10, if you do not order this you are mad." Show each dish as you talk about it. Keep it casual, keep it real.

Why it works: This format drives massive engagement because people disagree in the comments. "How is that only a 7?!" Comments and debates boost the video to new audiences. It also shows every single thing on your menu in one piece of content, and it lets your personality come through. People do not just eat at restaurants. They eat at restaurants run by people they like.
Idea 05
"We run two restaurants from one kitchen. Here is how."
Behind The Scenes Growth Story Day In The Life

Show the logistics of running Consett and Blackhall Mill. Morning prep for both locations. Loading up for the second site. Setting up at Riverview Bakery. Serving customers in two different places on the same night. The hustle. The chaos. The satisfaction when both locations are packed.

Why it works: "Day in the life" and "small business hustle" content is hugely popular because it is aspirational and relatable at the same time. People respect the work. They root for the growth. And it positions The Giddy Goose as a brand that is going somewhere, not just another local pizza shop. That perception drives loyalty and word-of-mouth.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the social media work.

Complete the Website

Your website is live but it is missing the most important thing a restaurant website needs: the menu. Someone who finds thegiddygoose.co.uk right now sees that you exist but cannot see what you serve, what it costs, or how to book. That is a dead end.

  • Full menu with prices, descriptions, and photos of hero dishes
  • Bottomless brunch page with details, pricing, and a booking button
  • Both locations clearly listed with addresses, hours, and maps
  • Online booking integration so people can reserve a table at any time
  • Direct ordering for delivery and collection rather than sending people to Just Eat (where you lose commission)

Quick win: Even just adding your menu as a PDF or simple page would immediately reduce the number of people who land on your site and leave without taking action. Every day without a menu page is lost revenue.

Own Your Delivery Channel

Right now, delivery orders go through Just Eat. That means you are paying commission on every order and you do not own the customer relationship. Just Eat keeps the customer data, not you. If you added direct ordering to your own website, you keep 100% of the revenue and you build a customer list you can market to again and again.

A simple ordering page on thegiddygoose.co.uk with a delivery radius around Consett would pay for itself within the first week. Promote it on social media and gradually shift customers away from the third-party platforms.

Google Business Profile for the Restaurant

Your five-star reviews were built on the trailer. The restaurant at 26 Newmarket Street needs its own established Google Business Profile with regular photo uploads, responses to every review, weekly posts about specials and events, and accurate hours for both locations.

  • Upload professional food photos weekly to keep the listing fresh
  • Respond to every single review, positive and negative
  • Post weekly updates about specials, events, and the bottomless brunch
  • Ensure both locations have accurate, verified listings

Bottomless Brunch Landing Page

Create a dedicated page specifically for your bottomless brunch offer. Optimise it for search terms like "bottomless brunch Consett," "bottomless brunch County Durham," and "bottomless brunch near me." Include pricing, what is included, photos of the experience, and a direct booking button. This single page could rank on Google and drive bookings from people who have never heard of The Giddy Goose but are actively searching for exactly what you offer.
