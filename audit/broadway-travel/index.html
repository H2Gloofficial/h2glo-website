What Is Already Working Well
Credit Where It Is Due

✓ TikTok is your strongest channel and it is bigger than your Instagram

31.3K TikTok followers vs 14K on Instagram is unusual in travel, and it is a very good sign. It means your TikTok content is actually reaching new audiences, while Instagram is mostly talking to people who already know the brand. Most travel companies have the opposite problem. You are already winning on the platform that matters most for discovery. The question is how to pour fuel on that fire.

✓ 77 years of brand equity is an enormous moat

Broadway was founded in 1948. That is older than most of the holidays you sell. In a market full of "TikTok travel agents" who popped up in 2023, trust like that is a genuine competitive advantage. It should be front and centre in the content, not hidden in an About page.

✓ The product range is a content engine waiting to happen

Short haul, long haul, cruise, city breaks, all inclusive, Beds With Ease for hotel-only. Every destination in the world is an excuse to make a video. Most agencies specialise because they cannot cover everything. You already cover everything, which means you never run out of content ideas.

✓ 250+ staff across three UK offices is a personality goldmine

Luton, Frimley, Newcastle. That is hundreds of travel agents, each with their own favourite destination, funniest booking story, and honest opinion on which resort is overrated. That human knowledge is the single most under-used asset in travel marketing, and you have more of it than almost any competitor.

✓ Cruise is a clear sub-brand opportunity

You already spun out @broadway_cruise. The cruise audience is distinct, older, bigger spend per booking, and genuinely underserved on short-form video. Doubling down on cruise content (ship reveals, cabin tours, first-timer advice) is a category almost nobody in the UK is owning yet.

Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

Broadway has the heritage, the staff, and the product range. The gaps are about how that story gets told on camera. Fix these and you widen the lead on every smaller competitor.

1

Instagram Is Static When It Should Be Short-Form Video

1,345 posts and 14K followers suggests Instagram has been treated as a place to drop holiday deals rather than to tell stories. In 2026, Instagram is a video-first platform. Reels get pushed, static carousels do not. The TikTok audience you already have is proof that your team can make video that travels. You just are not putting that muscle behind Instagram yet.

The fix is simple: take the same vertical video content you are already shooting for TikTok and post it to Reels within the hour. Broadway could double Instagram reach inside 90 days without filming a single extra video.

2

Agents Are Invisible, But Agents Are the Whole Product

The reason anyone books with Broadway rather than Booking.com or Expedia is human expertise. Yet the content barely features the people who provide it. There is a huge gap between "award-winning independent travel company" and "Kelly from the Newcastle team who has been to Antigua eleven times and will tell you the three resorts to avoid."

Agent-led content (destination recommendations, honest reviews, Q&A, "agent rates this resort") is the highest-trust, highest-converting format in travel and nobody in the UK independent space is owning it yet.

3

Customer Reactions Are Pure Gold and Unused

Every booking you take ends with a family having the best week of their year. Those moments of arrival, the first drink by the pool, the reaction when they see the room, the honeymoon surprise, they are already happening. They are being filmed by the customers themselves. Broadway has no visible system for collecting and resharing that content.

A branded hashtag (you already use #broadwaytravel, it just needs activating), pre-trip prompts in confirmation emails, and a simple UGC pipeline would turn every booking into content marketing for the next booking. Free, endless, and more persuasive than any polished ad.

4

Cruise Is a Billion-Pound Category With Zero Social Leaders

UK cruise bookings are at record highs. The audience skews older, spends more, and books further out than any other travel segment. And yet cruise content on TikTok and Reels is dominated by individual travel creators, not travel companies. @broadway_cruise at 6K followers is ahead of most competitors by default, but it is nowhere near its ceiling.

A dedicated cruise content strategy (cabin reveals, ship walkthroughs, first-time cruiser guides, itinerary breakdowns) could make Broadway the go-to UK cruise brand on social inside 12 months. The competition simply is not showing up.

5

Paid Social Is Almost Certainly Under-Producing

Broadway is a big enough advertiser that paid social is already part of the mix. But "deal post boosted" is a very different thing to "10 thumb-stopping creatives per week, all tested against each other, all optimised for cost per booking." The brands winning travel ads right now are running 50+ ad variants a month, almost all vertical video, almost all built from organic content that has already proven itself.

Pair the organic content engine with a paid testing framework and the CPA on bookings drops, often significantly. The two sides are not separate, they are the same engine.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats working right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels, built specifically around what makes Broadway unique: the destinations, the agents, and the moments that happen around a booking.

Idea 01
"Destination reveal: the holiday nobody thinks to book"
Reveal Destination Inspiration

Open with a Broadway agent straight to camera: "Everyone books Spain, Greece, Turkey. Here is the one you are sleeping on." Fast cuts of the destination (beach, food, hotel, local market, sunset). Agent returns: "Seven nights all inclusive from 489 pounds. And nobody is talking about it." End with a swipe to the Broadway link. Do this as a series. One destination per week.

Why it works: "Place you should know about" content is one of the top-performing travel formats. Agent-as-host gives instant authority that an anonymous brand channel cannot. Pricing on screen kills the biggest objection before viewers scroll. This single series could carry you for a year.
Idea 02
"Behind the scenes of a booking day at Broadway HQ"
Agents BTS Personality

A day in the life at Newcastle Business Park. Morning huddle, the first big booking of the day, a surprise request from a customer ("my wife wants to go somewhere she has never been"), an agent solving it in real time, the screen showing the hold confirmation, high-fives across the floor at a big cruise sale. Set to a feelgood trending sound. Frame it like sales floor energy, because that is what it is.

Why it works: People book travel agents for the human element. Showing the human element on camera is the most direct way to prove that value exists. It also makes Broadway look busy, successful, and full of experts, which is exactly the signal prospects need. Recruiting benefit is a bonus.
Idea 03
"Agents rank the resorts honestly: which ones to book, which ones to avoid"
Agent-Led Ranking High Trust

Two or three agents, side by side, ranking hotels in a specific destination. "Benidorm. I am going to tell you the truth." Brutally honest, fast-cut, no PR speak. Mix in short clips of each resort. End with "this is what I would book." Can be done for any destination, any price point, any customer type. Endless series potential.

Why it works: Honest ranking content drives massive engagement because it gives viewers something no brochure does: a real opinion. It differentiates Broadway from OTAs (Booking.com would never say one of their hotels is bad). And it positions agents as the experts they actually are.
Idea 04
"Things customers say when they arrive at their holiday"
Customer UGC Social Proof

Compilation of real Broadway customers filming themselves arriving at their hotel or on board their cruise ship. The first look at the pool, the view from the balcony, the first cocktail by the beach. Light text overlay: "Booked with Broadway." Set to a feelgood holiday track. Ask every customer to film 10 seconds on arrival in exchange for entry into a monthly prize draw.

Why it works: Reaction content is the highest-converting social proof in travel. Every clip answers the unspoken question "will my holiday actually be good?" The prize-draw mechanic gives you a constant, free pipeline of user-generated content that would cost thousands to produce otherwise.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

These are strategic changes that would compound the impact of the content work.

Cross-Post TikTok to Instagram Reels on Day One

You already have 31.3K engaged TikTok followers and proven content. Instagram Reels rewards that same vertical, hook-led style. Right now you are leaving half the reach on the table by posting different content on each platform.

  • Re-upload every TikTok to Instagram Reels within 60 minutes (without the watermark)
  • Convert top-performing Reels back to Story sequences for the existing Instagram audience
  • Track which hooks work on which platform to learn the audience difference between the two

Quick win: This single change often doubles Instagram impressions inside 60 days with zero additional production cost.

Turn Customer Confirmations Into a UGC Pipeline

Every booking triggers a confirmation email. That email is currently transactional. It should be the start of the content loop. A single line asking customers to film 10 seconds on arrival (for a prize draw, a discount off their next trip, or a simple shout-out) would generate more authentic travel content than any production budget could buy.

  • Add a "film your arrival" prompt to every booking confirmation
  • Include a QR code on the e-ticket that opens a 10-second upload form
  • Run a monthly "holiday of the month" winner to incentivise better clips
  • Reshare the best ones on @broadway_travel and @broadway_cruise

Position Cruise as the Flagship Sub-Brand

The cruise market is growing faster than any other UK travel segment and social content around cruise is still wide open. @broadway_cruise at 6K is a head start most competitors do not have. Investing in cruise-specific content (ship reveals, cabin tours, first-time cruiser advice, agent cruise picks) could make it your fastest-growing channel inside 12 months.

  • Dedicate at least two videos per week to cruise, split between TikTok and Reels
  • Partner with cruise lines for filming access when your agents sail for training
  • Build a "first-time cruiser" content series, that audience searches heavily and converts well

Pair Organic With a Paid Testing Framework

Every organic video that hits a threshold (say 100K views or a strong CTR in stories) should be promoted as a paid ad inside 48 hours. That is how travel brands like Jet2 and TUI compound wins. The winning creatives are almost always organic content repurposed, not bespoke ad production.

The opportunity: A structured creative testing loop typically cuts cost per booking by 25 to 40 percent within the first quarter. For a brand at Broadway's booking volume, that is a meaningful line-item.
