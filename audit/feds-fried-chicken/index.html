Ready-Made Content
6 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are performing right now on TikTok and Instagram Reels. Every one of them plays to what makes FEDs unique.

Idea 01
"How we brine our chicken in sweet tea"
ASMR Educational Process

Show the sweet tea brine being made. The chicken going in. Time-lapse of the brining. Then the dredging, the fry, and the final mopping in sauce. The entire journey from raw to plate, with close-up audio of every step. Let the process tell the story.

Why it works: Process content is one of the highest performing food formats online. Sweet tea brining is unusual and immediately intriguing. People will watch because they are curious, and they will share because it is genuinely interesting. This positions FEDs as craft fried chicken, not just another chicken shop.
Idea 02
"First bite reactions on Grey Street"
Reactions Social Proof Street

Hand customers a piece of chicken outside the shop and film their first bite. The crunch, the expression, the genuine reaction. Grey Street is the perfect backdrop. Quick cuts of multiple people reacting. No script, just real reactions to incredible food.

Why it works: First bite reactions are one of the most engaging food formats on TikTok. They are authentic, shareable, and create FOMO. When viewers see real people having genuine reactions, they want to try it themselves. The Grey Street setting adds visual appeal and location recognition for anyone in Newcastle.
Idea 03
"From Grainger Market to Grey Street"
Origin Story Brand Growth

Tell the FEDs story visually. Started during COVID in Grainger Market. Built a cult following from a market stall. Now on Grey Street. Photos or footage of the early days, the growth, the community, then the reveal of the new location. "Same chicken. Bigger dream."

Why it works: Origin stories create emotional connection. People root for independent businesses that grow from humble beginnings. This humanises the brand and gives new customers a reason to care about FEDs beyond the food. It is the kind of content that gets shared widely because people love a good underdog success story.
Idea 04
"Ranking every FEDs sauce"
Interactive Comments Debate

Line up every house-made sauce. Rank them from bottom to top. Be controversial. Put a fan favourite low. The comments section will explode. "We ranked our sauces and the internet lost it." Follow up with a customer vote version a week later.

Why it works: Ranking content drives comments and saves like almost nothing else. People feel compelled to share their own opinion, which pushes the video to more people through the algorithm. A controversial ranking guarantees engagement. The follow-up video doubles the content from one idea.
Idea 05
"Midnight chicken on Grey Street"
Late Night Energy Fri + Sat

You are open until midnight on Friday and Saturday. Film the late night crowd. The queue, the neon, the kitchen firing on all cylinders, people eating fried chicken on Grey Street at midnight. Fast cuts, high energy, the buzz of a Friday night. "This is FEDs at midnight."

Why it works: Late night food content has a massive audience. It taps into the weekend energy that people crave. This positions FEDs as the go-to late night spot in Newcastle and creates FOMO for anyone scrolling at home on a Friday night. The visual contrast of bright, golden chicken against a dark Grey Street backdrop is cinematic.
Idea 06
"Staff challenges and personality content"
Personality Fun Brand

Who can eat the hottest sauce without flinching? Speed challenges in the kitchen. The team rating each other's orders. Behind the scenes banter. Let the team's personality shine through. People follow people, not logos. Show the humans behind FEDs.

Why it works: Personality-driven content builds the strongest connections with audiences. When customers feel like they know the people behind the brand, they become loyal fans rather than just customers. Staff content is also easy to produce, endlessly repeatable, and tends to perform well because it feels authentic and unscripted.
