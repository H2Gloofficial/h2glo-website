Where The Opportunity Is
The Follower Count Does Not Match the Venue

The Pin is a premium, multi-activity entertainment hub with a nationally recognised hotel brand behind it. But with only 6,696 Instagram followers, the digital presence does not reflect the scale or quality of what is actually there. Here is where the biggest opportunities are.

1

6,696 Followers for a Venue This Size Is a Missed Opportunity

A multi-activity entertainment venue with a 43-bay driving range, bowling, darts, shuffleboard, and a sports bar should have a social following many times this size. Comparable entertainment venues across the UK with far less to offer are pulling in 20,000 to 50,000+ followers. The content potential is enormous, but it is not being captured and distributed in a way that grows an audience.

Every bay, every bowling lane, every game of darts is a content opportunity. Groups having fun, celebrations, competitive moments, food and drink being served. This kind of content performs exceptionally well on Instagram and TikTok because it is visual, energetic, and inherently shareable. The raw material is there every single day. It just needs to be filmed and posted consistently.

2

No Dedicated TikTok Presence

TikTok is the most powerful platform for entertainment and experience-based venues right now. People search TikTok for "things to do in Durham," "best night out near me," and "fun group activities." If The Pin is not showing up in those searches, potential customers are finding competitors instead.

Entertainment content is TikTok gold. Bowling strikes, driving range trick shots, competitive darts moments, group reactions. These are the exact formats that go viral on the platform. A single well-filmed bowling strike compilation could reach hundreds of thousands of people in the North East without spending a penny on ads. The Pin is sitting on a content goldmine and leaving it completely untouched.

3

Short-Form Video Content Is Minimal

The most effective way to grow on Instagram and TikTok in 2026 is through short-form video. Reels, TikToks, Shorts. These formats get pushed to people who do not already follow you, which is how you grow. Static images and occasional posts do not have the same reach or impact.

With the range of activities at The Pin, you could be posting multiple pieces of video content every week, each showcasing a different experience. A 15-second clip of a group celebrating a bowling strike. A Toptracer leaderboard challenge. A slow-motion dart hitting the bullseye. A packed sports bar during a big match. Each of these is a chance to reach thousands of new potential customers.

4

"Things to Do in Durham" Positioning

When people search for activities in Durham, whether locals looking for a night out or visitors staying in the area, The Pin should be one of the first results they see on social media. Right now, that is not happening. The combination of driving range, bowling, darts, shuffleboard, and a sports bar makes The Pin one of the most complete entertainment offerings in the region. But if the content is not there, people do not find it.

Creating content that directly targets searches like "things to do in Durham," "best group activities North East," and "fun nights out Durham" would position The Pin as the go-to entertainment destination. This is organic discovery that brings in new customers week after week, month after month.

5

Food and Drink Is an Untapped Content Angle

The sports bar at The Pin serves food and drinks, and this is an entire content category that most entertainment venues overlook. People love food and drink content. A perfectly pulled pint, a sharing platter arriving at a table, cocktails being made. Combined with the entertainment context, "drinks and bowling" or "food and driving range," it adds another dimension to the experience and another reason for people to visit.

Food and drink reveals are some of the highest-performing content formats on both Instagram and TikTok. Showing what people can eat and drink while they bowl, play darts, or hit the range adds value to every other piece of content you create.

Ready-Made Content
6 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working right now for entertainment venues on TikTok and Instagram. Every one of them plays to The Pin's strengths.

Idea 01
"Bowling strike compilation at The Pin"
Bowling Compilation TikTok Gold

Film from behind the lanes over a busy weekend. Capture every strike. Slow motion. Reactions. Group celebrations. Cut them together into a fast-paced 30-second compilation with a trending audio track. End with "The Pin at Ramside Hall. Book your lane."

Why it works: Bowling strike compilations are one of the most reliable viral formats on TikTok. The satisfying visual of pins scattering combined with genuine group reactions creates content people watch on repeat and share with friends. "We need to go here" is the exact comment you want.
Idea 02
"Can anyone beat the Toptracer leaderboard?"
Driving Range Challenge Interactive

Show the current top score on the Toptracer leaderboard. Film someone attempting to beat it. The swing, the ball tracking on screen, the distance, the reaction. Make it a recurring series. "This week's challenger." Invite people to come and try to take the top spot.

Why it works: Challenge and leaderboard content drives engagement because people want to prove they can do better. It creates a reason to visit beyond just hitting balls. It turns the driving range into a competitive, shareable experience and gives you a recurring content series that practically runs itself.
Idea 03
"POV: group night out at The Pin, Durham"
Experience Social Proof Atmosphere

Follow a group through an entire evening at The Pin. Start at the driving range. Move to bowling. Play some darts. Hit the shuffleboard. End at the sports bar with drinks and food. Fast cuts, energy, laughter. No voiceover needed, just let the atmosphere sell the experience.

Why it works: This is the ultimate "I want to do that" content. Showing the full breadth of what The Pin offers in one 30-second video makes people realise just how much there is to do. It positions The Pin as the complete night out, not just one activity. Group tagging in the comments drives reach organically.
Idea 04
"Things to do in Durham that are not just a pub"
Local Discovery SEO Durham

Quick-fire showcase of The Pin's activities with text overlays. "43-bay Toptracer driving range." "6-lane bowling alley." "AR darts." "Interactive shuffleboard." "Sports bar." Each one gets 3 seconds. End with the location and a booking prompt. Clean, punchy, informative.

Why it works: This format directly targets people searching "things to do in Durham" on TikTok and Instagram. It is discovery content designed to reach people who have never heard of The Pin. The quick-fire format holds attention, and the variety of activities makes people save the video for later. Saves are one of the strongest signals for the algorithm to push content further.
Idea 05
"The sports bar during the match"
Sports Bar Atmosphere Event

Film the sports bar during a big football match or sporting event. The screens, the crowd, the reactions to a goal, drinks being poured, the energy. Capture the roar. Use it to promote the next big match viewing. "Where are you watching the game this weekend?"

Why it works: Sports atmosphere content taps into an enormous, passionate audience. People are always looking for the best place to watch the match, and a video showing a buzzing sports bar with great screens and a good crowd is the best advert you can create. Post it the day before the next big fixture and watch the bookings come in.
Idea 06
"Food and drink reveal at The Pin"
Food Drink Reveal

Close-up shots of food arriving at the table. Burgers, sharing platters, cocktails, pints. Slow-motion pours. Steam rising. Cheese pulls. Cut between the food and the activity happening in the background. Bowling lanes, the driving range, people having fun. "This is not just a night out. This is The Pin."

Why it works: Food content consistently performs well, and when you pair it with the entertainment context, it becomes even more compelling. People do not just want to eat. They want to eat somewhere exciting. Showing food and drink alongside bowling and golf positions The Pin as a full experience, not just an activity. It drives higher spend per visit.
