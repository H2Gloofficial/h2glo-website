What Is Already Working Well
Credit Where It Is Due

✓ Fenwick is a top-tier umbrella brand to launch under

Fenwick has been around since 1882 and the Newcastle flagship carries serious cultural weight in the North East. The Christmas window alone is a national moment. Haze does not need to explain who it is, the Fenwick name does that work for free. Most new bars spend years building trust that Haze inherits on day one.

✓ 165K combined Instagram audience already exists above it

@fenwickofficial has a 165K Instagram following and strong local engagement. That audience is already paying attention to anything Fenwick Newcastle launches. Haze can be surfaced to a warm, qualified, local audience the second it has consistent content to push. Most independent bars would kill for that kind of launch platform.

✓ Rooftop plus luxury retail is a genuinely unique combo

There is no real equivalent in Newcastle. Vermont has ABOVE, but the Fenwick context is different. You are drinking on top of a luxury department store on Northumberland Street. That gives Haze a specific aesthetic, shopping day, treat culture, after-work glamour, birthday and hen crowd, that other rooftops in the city cannot mirror.

✓ Cocktail programme has credibility baked in

Fenwick's rooftop bar programme has worked with names like Joe Schofield and Hepple Spirits in the past. That credibility translates on camera. When a bartender has a real story, the cocktail content almost makes itself. The ingredients are there, they just need to be filmed the way the algorithm rewards in 2026.

✓ Foot traffic inside Fenwick is a built-in funnel

Thousands of people walk through Fenwick every day. Shoppers, tourists, business lunches, hen parties, post-work crowds. Haze has one of the most valuable pre-qualified footfall funnels of any bar in Newcastle. The job is simply making sure they know the rooftop exists and making them want to film it when they get there.

Where The Opportunity Is
Five Areas With Real Potential

The venue is special. The brand is iconic. The gap is almost entirely in how the story is being told online. Fix these and the growth will follow.

1

No Dedicated Social Identity for Haze

Right now, content about the rooftop is folded into the wider Fenwick feed, which is fashion, beauty, Christmas, events and retail all at once. That dilutes the rooftop story. A bar needs a clear, consistent, ownable identity: the crowd, the view, the cocktails, the energy at 7pm on a Friday. That is not something a parent retail feed can deliver on its own.

A dedicated @fenwickhaze handle on Instagram and TikTok, cross-promoted from the main Fenwick accounts, would give the rooftop its own voice. The main Fenwick audience becomes the launchpad, not the ceiling. Within six months, Haze could have a stand-alone following that works for its own bookings without relying on the parent feed.

2

TikTok Is a Wide Open Door

Fenwick's own TikTok sits at 10.3K followers. For a 140-year-old national retail brand that is small, and for a rooftop bar it is almost nothing. Rooftop bars, cocktail pours, reveal content, and luxury retail interiors are all top-performing TikTok categories. Haze sits at the exact intersection of those.

A single well-filmed sunset reveal reel from the top of Fenwick, posted with a trending sound, could comfortably clear half a million views. The platform is still the cheapest place in hospitality to grow organically, and there is no competitor in Newcastle owning the "rooftop cocktail" search on TikTok yet. That should be Haze.

3

The Luxury Department Store Context Is Barely Used

The Fenwick interior is a visual gift. Escalators, fragrance hall, Christmas windows, fashion floors, then the reveal onto the rooftop. That entire journey is content most bars simply cannot create. Right now that vertical experience is not being turned into video. Viewers just see the bar itself, they do not see the five-floor build-up that makes Haze feel like an occasion.

Filming the journey from the ground floor to the rooftop, cut tight with a trending sound, is a format that routinely performs in the hundreds of thousands on TikTok. The Fenwick context is the whole point. Lean into it.

4

No Obvious UGC or Creator Strategy

Rooftop bars are one of the most-filmed categories in hospitality. Customers at Haze are already creating content when they visit. The view, the cocktail in hand, the friend across the table. But there is no visible branded hashtag, no consistent resharing of customer stories, and no creator seeding programme targeting Newcastle-based lifestyle accounts.

A simple creator strategy, five to ten local accounts invited per month for cocktails in exchange for one Reel or TikTok, would multiply reach without increasing production cost. Combine that with a branded hashtag on table cards and Haze could collect free qualified content every single week.

5

Review Narratives Need Countering With Content

Some TripAdvisor reviews of the Fenwick rooftop flag pricing, service speed and value. Fair or not, people read those before they book. The most effective way to rewrite the narrative is not by arguing in the response thread. It is by putting out content that visibly shows the opposite.

Attentive staff, cocktails landing quickly, a warm welcome, a full table enjoying themselves at golden hour. Thirty seconds of that, shot well, outweighs twenty written review responses. Content is the most underrated reputation tool a hospitality brand has.

Ready-Made Content
4 Video Ideas You Could Film This Week

These are formats that are working on TikTok and Instagram Reels right now. All four lean directly into what makes Haze unique: rooftop, cocktails, and the luxury Fenwick context.

Idea 01
"Most people don't know there is a rooftop bar on top of Fenwick"
Hidden Gem Reveal Journey

Start at the ground floor fragrance hall. Quick cuts up through the fashion floors, maybe the food hall, the escalators, the lift. Each beat shows how deep inside the store you are going. Then a door opens, you step out, wide shot of the Newcastle skyline and a cocktail being placed on the bar. Text overlay: "This is hiding on top of Fenwick." Trending audio underneath.

Why it works: "Hidden gem" and vertical journey content are two of the most viral formats on TikTok right now. The Fenwick interior gives Haze a journey nobody else has. The payoff of the rooftop view makes it sharable. This single format alone could clear six figures in views the first time it is filmed properly.
Idea 02
"Ranking every cocktail on the Haze menu with the bartender"
Ranking Staff Personality

Get the head bartender on camera. Line every cocktail up along the bar with the Newcastle skyline behind them. Taste, rate, and rank each one honestly. Let them have strong opinions. Quick cuts, tight edits, each drink gets a reaction and a score. The winner gets a slow-motion hero shot against the view. Signature pour at golden hour.

Why it works: Ranking content is engagement gold because viewers argue in the comments. It showcases the full menu, puts a real face to the bar, and leans into Fenwick's cocktail credibility. It is also a series: signature drinks vs classics, summer menu, staff picks vs customer picks. Unlimited variations from one format.
Idea 03
"A day at Fenwick ending with sunset cocktails on the rooftop"
Aspirational Lifestyle Vlog

Frame the video as a perfect day out in Newcastle. Shopping in Fenwick, maybe a fragrance sample, a fitting room moment, a bite in the food hall, a beauty counter detail. Build the aesthetic. Then the final scene: golden hour, rooftop, cocktail, friend, skyline. Position Haze as the reward, the finale, the "we have to end the day here" moment.

Why it works: "Perfect day" content is one of the strongest performing lifestyle formats on Instagram Reels in 2026. It naturally targets the exact audience Haze wants, the fashion-aware, hospitality-loving, treat-yourself crowd. It also tightens the link between Fenwick shopping and Haze drinking, turning every shopper into a potential booking.
Idea 04
"POV: Your first cocktail at Newcastle's smartest rooftop"
POV Cocktail ASMR

Shot entirely from the customer's point of view. Walking through the door onto the rooftop, the view hits the frame. A hand places a menu. A finger points at a cocktail. The bartender builds it in front of the camera, close ups on the pour, the garnish, the ice. The drink lands. First sip. Wide reveal of the skyline behind it. Minimal speaking, lots of sound design.

Why it works: POV cocktail content combines reveal, ASMR and aspirational aesthetics in one format, all top-tier TikTok drivers. The Fenwick rooftop backdrop elevates it beyond what a regular bar could shoot. This is also the kind of content that gets saved, which is the strongest signal you can send the algorithm.
Digital Enhancements
Beyond Social Media

Strategic changes that would make a real difference alongside the content work.

Launch a Dedicated Haze Social Identity

A bar needs its own voice. Right now Haze lives inside a retail feed. Splitting it out into its own handles, cross-promoted from Fenwick, would let the rooftop story breathe and create a proper booking engine.

  • Secure @fenwickhaze on Instagram and TikTok before anyone else grabs it
  • Announce the launch from the main @fenwickofficial account to drive the first 5K followers overnight
  • Develop a visual identity distinct from Fenwick retail: warmer, slower, more editorial
  • Pin a strong reveal video to the grid so every new visitor immediately understands the venue

Quick win: Even before the dedicated account is live, a branded hashtag on table cards would start collecting UGC that can be fed straight into the launch day content bank.

Build a Creator Seeding Programme

Newcastle has a strong lifestyle and foodie creator scene. Haze is the kind of venue they are actively looking for. A structured seeding programme, not ad-hoc invites, would turn that into consistent reach.

  • Identify 15 to 20 Newcastle-based creators in lifestyle, fashion and food
  • Invite five to ten per month for cocktails in exchange for one Reel or TikTok
  • Track which creators drive the highest view counts and build longer term relationships with the top performers
  • Use the strongest UGC as boosted paid content for geo-targeted Newcastle ads

Own the "Rooftop Bar Newcastle" Search

People searching "rooftop bar Newcastle" on TikTok, Instagram and Google are already in market. The venue that owns those searches owns the default booking. Right now nobody in Newcastle properly owns it across all three.

  • SEO optimise a dedicated Haze page on fenwick.co.uk for "rooftop bar Newcastle" and related terms
  • Use consistent captions and hashtags on every TikTok and Reel to dominate platform search
  • Claim and optimise Google Business Profile, TripAdvisor and Yelp listings with fresh photos and video
  • Run a low-spend Google and Meta campaign targeting "things to do in Newcastle" and "cocktail bar Newcastle"

Turn Events Into Content Cycles

Any pop-ups, seasonal launches, private hires or collaborations should run on a three-part content cycle. Teaser before, atmosphere during, recap after. That turns every single event into a week of content rather than a single static post.

The reality: Haze sitting inside Fenwick means it benefits from the retail calendar. Summer menu, Christmas windows, pop-ups, brand collabs. Every one of those moments is free content fuel most bars do not have.
