How We Got a Restaurant 30 Million Views in 90 Days

Apr 14, 2026 · Jay Williams · 6 min read

We took New Greek, a small independent Greek restaurant in Heaton, Newcastle, from almost no social media presence to over 30 million views in 90 days. One single reel hit 30 million views on its own. The restaurant went from empty tables to fully booked with a waiting list.

And we did it by doing one simple thing: creating content that people actually want to watch.

The Problem Most Restaurants Have

Most restaurants approach social media the same way. They take photos of their food on a nice plate, write a caption about the daily special, post it, and wonder why nobody engages.

The issue is not the food. The food is probably great. The issue is that the content is not made for social media. It is made for a menu, not for someone scrolling TikTok at 11pm.

Nobody is opening their phone to be sold to. Nobody wakes up and thinks "I hope a restaurant tries to convince me to buy something today." People open their phones to be entertained. To laugh. To feel something. To kill time.

If your content does not fit into that, you do not exist on their feed.

The One Simple Thing

We created content that people want to watch. That is it. That is the entire strategy.

Not content that shows the menu. Not content that says "come visit us." Not content that looks like an advert. Content that entertains first and sells second.

We used a blend of two types of content:

1. Trending Content

Taking trending sounds, formats, and hooks from TikTok and Instagram and adapting them for the restaurant. This is how you get reach. The algorithm already wants to push these formats because they are proven to get engagement. You just need to put your own spin on them.

2. Promotional Content

Showing the food, the experience, the menu. But done in a way that fits naturally into someone's feed rather than feeling like an advert. Think "this looks incredible" not "please buy this."

The ratio matters. It is not 50/50. It is more like 80% entertainment, 20% promotion. The entertainment gets you in front of new people. The promotion converts them into customers.

The Funnel Logic

This is the part most people miss. Social media is the top of the funnel. The wider you make it, the more customers filter through to your business.

If 100 people see your content, maybe 5 visit your profile, maybe 2 check the menu, maybe 1 books a table.

If 1 million people see your content, those same percentages become very different numbers. 50,000 profile visits. 20,000 menu checks. 10,000 potential customers.

That is why reach matters. That is why views matter. Not as a vanity metric, but because the larger the top of the funnel, the more customers filter through to the business. Which means they make more money.

The Results

For New Greek, the results were:

  • 40M+ total views across Instagram and TikTok
  • 30M views on a single Instagram reel
  • 525K+ engagements
  • 10,000+ new followers grown organically
  • Fully booked restaurant with a waiting list
  • Custom website, booking system, and analytics dashboard built alongside the content

All from a small independent restaurant that had almost zero social media presence before we started.

What This Means for Your Business

If you run a restaurant, a cafe, a bar, or any hospitality business, the opportunity is sitting right in front of you. The content is there. The stories are there. The food is there. You just need someone who knows how to turn it into content that the algorithm wants to push and that people actually want to watch.

Stop posting like a business. Start creating like an entertainer. And watch what happens to your bookings.
